## HYDRATION STRATEGY

### Daily
- **Base:** 2.5-3 liters water per day (adjust for activity)
- **Electrolytes:** Include sodium in diet (sea salt, salted snacks) for retention

### During Rides
- **Short rides (<90 min):** Water only, 400-600 ml/hour
- **Long rides (>2 hours):** 500-750 ml/hour with 4-8% carb-electrolyte solution
- **Recipe:** 1 liter water + 40-60g sugar/honey + pinch of salt (homemade sports drink)
- **Temperature:** Cool drinks (12-15°C) in warm conditions

## KEY COACHING PRINCIPLES

### Periodization & Progressive Overload
- **Build volume gradually:** Never increase weekly volume by >10% per month
- **Vary intensity:** Mix Zone 2 steady, Zone 3 tempo, and shorter hard efforts
- **Tailor climbing practice:** Do sustained climbs in build phase; shorter, harder repeats later

### Listen to Your Body
- **Resting heart rate:** Elevated by 5+ bpm = need more recovery
- **Soreness:** Normal 24-48 hrs post-workout; persistent soreness suggests overtraining
- **Performance:** If pace feels hard at easy effort, take a recovery day

### Age 57 Considerations
- **Recovery prioritization:** At your age, recovery is more important than additional training volume
- **Strength training essential:** Maintains bone density and muscle mass (especially critical for cyclists)
- **Joint care:** Warm up longer (10-15 min); include mobility work daily
- **Mental resilience:** Your experience is an asset; use it for pacing strategy during the race

### Race Strategy for 160 km, 3000m+ Climbing
- **Start conservatively:** First 30 km is a shakedown; don't chase fast people
- **Climbing approach:** Break climbs into 15-20 min segments; recover between
- **Pacing sweet spot:** Aim for 12-13 km/h average on hills, 24-26 km/h on flats
- **Nutrition discipline:** Stick to your fueling plan; don't skip feeds
- **Mental toughness:** At km 120 (likely ~10 hrs in), focus on finishing, not pace
