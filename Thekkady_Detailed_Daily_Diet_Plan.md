## PART 6: PHASE-SPECIFIC ADJUSTMENTS

### **PHASE 1 (Weeks 1–6): BASE BUILDING**
- **Focus:** Consistency, aerobic foundation
- **Daily Target:** Maintain targets above (no increase needed)
- **Special Notes:** 
  - Emphasize rest days — 3 per week
  - Light trainer sessions (60 min easy)
  - No long rides yet

### **PHASE 2 (Weeks 7–14): CLIMB-SPECIFIC**
- **Focus:** Climbing power, sustained effort
- **Increase:** Add 50–100 extra calories on training days
  - Extra fruit (banana, apple, dates)
  - Extra 0.5 cup dal at lunch
- **Long Ride Days:** Start incorporating (100–110 km)
- **Magnesium Focus:** Add dark chocolate (jaggery-sweetened) + pumpkin seeds

### **PHASE 3 (Weeks 15–22): EVENT-SPECIFIC**
- **Focus:** Endurance, back-to-back efforts
- **Peak Long Rides:** 120–150 km
- **Long Ride Days:** 2–3 per week (use Long Ride Day plans above)
- **Extra Nutrition:** Increase on-bike fuelling practice
  - Test all homemade nutrition (ladoos, rice cakes, wraps)
- **Evening Recovery:** Add extra milk/curd post-long rides

### **PHASE 4 (Weeks 23–26): TAPER**
- **Focus:** Freshness, sharpness
- **Reduce:** Drop to 2000–2200 kcal on rest days; 2200–2400 on light training days
- **Long Rides:** Only 1 × 100 km in Week 24
- **Carb-Loading (Week 25):** Increase carbs to 600g on long ride day (stay at same macros, just increase rice/roti portions by 20%)

## PART 7: DAILY HYDRATION PROTOCOL

### **Daily Baseline (Every Day)**
- **Morning:** 2–3 glasses warm water with lemon juice (no sugar) upon waking
- **Throughout Day:** 3.5–4 litres minimum
- **On Training Days:** 4–5 litres minimum
- **On Long Ride Days:** 5+ litres total (including on-bike fluids)

### **Electrolyte Water Recipe (DIY — Better than Packaged)**
```
Ingredients:
- 500 ml water (filtered)
- 1/4 tsp rock salt (sendha namak) — adds sodium + minerals
- Juice of 1/2 lemon — adds potassium + vitamin C
- 1 tsp jaggery powder — adds glucose + minerals
- Optional: 1 pinch dry ginger powder (anti-inflammatory)

Preparation:
Mix all and drink during/after climbs (from 60 km onwards on long rides)
Make fresh daily; use within 8 hours
```

### **Coconut Water Alternative**
- Fresh coconut water: 200–300 ml as afternoon drink on training days
- Superior to any sports drink (natural electrolytes, potassium, magnesium)
- Available in Kerala year-round; buy from street vendors

### **Post-Ride Recovery Drink**
- **Immediately After Ride:** 200–300 ml coconut water + 1 banana
- **30 min Post-Ride:** Warm milk with turmeric + 1 tsp jaggery (haldi-doodh)
- **Avoid:** Carbonated drinks, packaged juices, energy drinks with artificial colours

## PART 8: MICRONUTRIENT FOCUS FOR 57-YEAR-OLD CYCLIST

### **Calcium** (Bone Density During Climbing Impacts)
- **Daily Requirement:** 1000–1200 mg
- **Best Sources:**
  - Ragi porridge (1.5 cups): ~150 mg
  - Sesame seeds (2 tbsp in ladoos/sabzi): ~150 mg
  - Almonds (15g soaked): ~40 mg
  - Milk/Curd (300 ml/day): ~300 mg
  - Paneer (80g, 2–3 times/week): ~200 mg
  - Spinach (palak) sabzi: ~100 mg
- **Target:** Aim for 800–900 mg/day from diet above

### **Iron** (Oxygen Transport, Anti-Fatigue)
- **Daily Requirement:** 8 mg
- **Best Sources:**
  - Moong/Masoor dal (1 cup): ~6 mg
  - Spinach (palak) sabzi (1 cup): ~2 mg
  - Dates (3–4 dried): ~1.5 mg
  - Dark chocolate jaggery-sweetened: ~1 mg
  - Ragi (1 cup): ~0.5 mg
- **Tip:** Pair with lemon juice (vitamin C aids iron absorption)

### **Magnesium** (Anti-Cramping, Critical for Climbs)
- **Daily Requirement:** 400–420 mg
- **Best Sources:**
  - Pumpkin seeds (kaddu ke beej, 2 tbsp): ~150 mg
  - Almonds (20g soaked): ~60 mg
  - Banana (1 medium): ~27 mg
  - Dark chocolate (30g, jaggery-sweetened): ~50 mg
  - Spinach (1 cup): ~50 mg
  - Cashews (occasional, 20g): ~45 mg
- **ON LONG RIDES:** Consume magnesium-rich foods from Phase 2 onwards

### **Potassium** (Electrolyte Balance, Cramp Prevention)
- **Best Sources:**
  - Banana (1 medium): ~358 mg
  - Coconut water (200 ml): ~300 mg
  - Sweet potato (1 medium): ~450 mg
  - Dates (3 dried): ~350 mg
- **Automatic:** Covered if you follow meal plans above

### **B12** (Critical — Pure Vegetarian Must Monitor)
- **Vegetarian Deficiency:** High risk in plant-only diets
- **Recommendation:** 
  - Consult your doctor for B12 blood test
  - Consider supplement: 500 mcg sublingual B12, 2–3 times/week
  - Milk/Curd provide some B12, but unreliable amounts
- **Symptoms of Deficiency:** Fatigue, weakness, tingling — unacceptable for 160 km ride

### **Vitamin D**
- **Source:** 20–30 minutes morning sun exposure before trainer ride
- **No Food Substitute:** Vegan/vegetarian foods have minimal Vitamin D
- **Alternative:** Ask doctor about D3 supplement (1000–2000 IU, if blood test shows deficiency)

- **4:45 AM:** Ragi porridge (1.5 cups) + 1 banana + dates

- **Wait:** Full 60 minutes if possible (outdoor rides are intense)

### **Before Evening Indoor Session (Post-Lunch)**
- **3:00 PM Snack:** 1 banana + 1 tbsp peanut butter on small roti
- **Wait:** 45 minutes
- **Session Start:** 4:00 PM

## PART 10: SPECIAL SCENARIOS & ADJUSTMENTS

### **If You Gain Body Weight During Phase 2–3**
- **Check:** Are you eating more than macros allow?
- **Adjust:** Reduce snack portions slightly (e.g., 10 almonds → 8 almonds)
- **Increase:** Long ride frequency (burns 2000–2500 kcal per 150 km ride)
- **Goal:** Stay within 81–83 kg for event

### **If You Feel Fatigued During Training**
- **Possible Cause 1:** Under-fuelling on training days
  - **Solution:** Add extra 200 kcal (banana + peanut butter) post-trainer
- **Possible Cause 2:** Insufficient recovery sleep
  - **Solution:** Aim for 7–8 hours, ensure warm milk before bed
- **Possible Cause 3:** B12 deficiency (see Part 8)
  - **Solution:** Get blood test, start supplement

### **If You Develop Saddle Soreness (Trainer Overuse)**
- **Reduce:** Indoor trainer days by 1 per week
- **Increase:** Outdoor miles instead
- **Use:** Chamois cream (Himalaya brand available)
- **Get:** Professional saddle height check (Bangalore cycle shops: Bikerides.in, Pro Cycling)

### **If You Experience Cramping During Long Rides**
- **Phase 2 Onwards:** Increase magnesium foods (see Part 8)
- **DIY Electrolyte:** Use recipe in Part 7, start sipping from 60 km
- **On Bike:** Consume every 40 km (don't wait for thirst)
- **Evening Before:** Increase curd/paneer intake

### **Race Day (Tour of Thekkady)**
- **Night Before:** 
  - Rice + moong dal + light sabzi + curd (nothing fancy)
  - 1 banana 90 min before sleep
- **Morning of Event:**
  - Ragi porridge + 2 dates + jaggery (same as training mornings)
  - Start eating at 4:30 AM if flag-off is 5:30 AM
- **On Bike:** Stick to tested nutrition from training (no new foods)
